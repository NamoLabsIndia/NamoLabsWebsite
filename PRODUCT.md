# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Highly-skilled technical candidates (senior engineers, ML researchers, cryptographers, blockchain developers) evaluating whether Namo Labs is the right next move. They are evaluating the company as seriously as the company is evaluating them. Secondary audience: enterprise partners and investors researching the company.

## Product Purpose

The Namo Labs website communicates the company's identity, mission, and credibility as a serious deep-tech research lab. The careers page specifically must win the trust and imagination of top-tier technical talent. Success looks like a qualified candidate feeling genuine excitement and confidence that this is a place worthy of their skills.

## Brand Personality

Expert confidence. Research gravity. Purposeful restraint.

The brand is not a startup trying to look cool. It is a serious research-and-engineering organisation that builds foundational infrastructure — cryptography, AI, blockchain, quantum. Tone is measured, precise, and quietly ambitious. Not loud, not playful, not corporate. Closest physical object: a well-designed academic proceedings volume with a strong cover — precise typography, weight, and a single committed accent colour.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS startup (Stripe, Linear, Notion clones): too product-market-fit, too conversion-funnel
- Overworked enterprise corporate (IBM, Accenture style): too cold, too committee
- Trendy AI-startup cream/sand aesthetic (the 2026 warm-neutral flood): too warm, reads AI-generated by default
- Loud crypto/web3 maximalism: too speculative, undermines research credibility

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over assertion.** Show real people, real work, real specifics. Avoid marketing superlatives that any company could claim.
2. **Precision is a feature.** Typography, spacing, and copywriting at the same standard as the engineering. Sloppiness in the interface contradicts the brand claim.
3. **Quiet ambition.** The work is serious. The page should feel serious without being intimidating. Gravitas, not hype.
4. **Earn trust, don't demand it.** Every section should give the candidate something specific — a real belief, a real principle, a real person — not a generic promise.
5. **One voice throughout.** The page is a single continuous argument for why a talented person should choose Namo Labs. Every section must advance that argument, not just exist.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced-motion support expected (framer-motion already handles this). Keyboard navigation required for all interactive elements, especially the role filter tabs and application form.
